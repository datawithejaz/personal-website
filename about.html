About me

A bit about me

I'm a data and advertising analyst based in London. My work sits at the intersection of performance marketing and data science — I've spent the last six years running campaigns for brands across FMCG, retail, education, automotive, and wellness, and I'm increasingly using Python and ML to do it more precisely.

I hold an MSc in Data Science & AI from the University of Suffolk (2025) and a BBA in Marketing from IOBM, Karachi (2019). The MSc wasn't a career pivot — it was a deliberate move to sharpen the analytical layer on top of the marketing experience I'd already built.

I've worked agency-side at WPP Media — progressing through three roles in under three years while managing the Unilever digital portfolio across seven brands, at Publicis across 26+ brand accounts, and independently as a consultant. I also ran my own digital marketing agency for three years — 46 clients across 16 industry sectors — before moving into larger agency networks. More recently, I've taken on freelance consulting in DTC e-commerce, building paid acquisition channels from scratch for brands on Shopify.

Outside of client work, I run Data with Ejaz — a content brand where I write and create about advertising analytics, AI in marketing, and career in data. I try to be the voice in the room that asks "does this data actually support that conclusion?" — because in marketing, it often doesn't.
